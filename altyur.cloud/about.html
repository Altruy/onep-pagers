Who we are

hytGenX is a US-registered AI systems engineering firm. We build production AI infrastructure for mid-market enterprises — organisations between 50 and 150 people that have reached the point where manual operations are measurably limiting growth, but have not yet built an internal AI team to address it.

We work in logistics, legal, FinTech, and professional services. These are environments where process complexity is high, data is sensitive, and the cost of a system that does not run reliably in production is significant. Our clients cannot afford demos. They need systems that work on Monday morning.

Our approach is what we call Glass Box AI. Every system we build is transparent, documented, and auditable. The AI runs inside your environment — your AWS account, your Azure tenant, your on-premise servers. hytGenX has no ongoing access to your operational data. You own the code, you control the infrastructure, and you can explain what the system does to anyone who needs to know. We build systems that run inside your environment. Not ours.

We work with a small number of clients at a time. Every engagement gets senior, direct attention. Most new relationships come through referral.

About our CTO

Turyal Neeshat leads every client engagement at hytGenX. He is the architect on every build and the primary point of contact throughout the engagement — from the first discovery session through to production handover.

Turyal spent years inside business operations before AI became a realistic option for most companies. He watched finance teams burn Monday mornings pulling numbers from five different systems just to produce a report that would be stale by the time it reached leadership. He watched sales teams lose deals not because they were outcompeted but because their follow-up process could not keep pace with their pipeline. He watched founders make strategic decisions on week-old data because the people who could produce current data were too busy producing last week's version.

The tools existed. The processes were documented. The people were capable. The connection was always missing.

After 50+ production AI systems, the pattern that determines success or failure is almost never technical. It is whether the team did the process documentation work before building anything. Most AI automation projects fail because they start with a tool and work backwards. The right question is: what is the actual constraint in this workflow, and what would it take to remove it? That answer determines the tool — not the other way around.

Turyal cares about systems that run in production. There is an enormous amount of AI automation that works in demos and breaks in the real world — because it does not account for exceptions, because it has no error handling, because it was built for the happy path and the real world is not the happy path. Every system shipped by hytGenX has monitoring, exception routing, documentation, and a structured handover process. Six months after the engagement ends, your team should be able to extend it, explain it to a new hire, or show it to an auditor. That is what production means.
